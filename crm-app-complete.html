💻 Terminal Menu Script

Save this as prompts.sh in your project root, then run ./prompts.sh

#!/bin/bash

echo "🚀 Website Demo Build — Select a Prompt"
echo ""
echo "Phase 1: Strategy"
echo "  1) Pitch Demo Strategy"
echo "  2) Source & Placeholder Kit"
echo ""
echo "Phase 2: Build"
echo "  3) Competitor Look & Feel"
echo "  4) Design System & Scaffold"
echo "  5) Homepage (WOW Moment) ⭐ PAUSE HERE"
echo "  6) Shop Category Page"
echo "  7) Enquiry Page"
echo "  8) Local Authority Page"
echo ""
echo "Phase 3: Deploy"
echo "  9) Polish Pass"
echo " 10) Deploy & Pitch"
echo ""
read -p "Select (1-10): " choice

case $choice in
  [1-9]|10)
    file="./Products/.claude/prompts/$(printf "%02d" "$choice")-"*.md
    if [[ -f $file ]]; then
      cat "$file" | pbcopy && echo "✓ Copied to clipboard"
    else
      echo "❌ Prompt file not found for option $choice"
    fi
    ;;
  *)
    echo "❌ Invalid choice"
    ;;
esac

📋 Phase 1: Strategy (30-45 min)

Prompt 1 — Pitch Demo Strategy

Plan your demo approach

What to do: Paste this prompt to explain the demo strategy to Claude.
How: Copy the prompt, go to Claude Code, paste it, and let it review the strategy.
Output: Claude will confirm it understands the three pillars and outline the approach.
⚠️ Important: This is a DEMO only — no real payments, no live Shopify, content is indicative.

Prompt 2 — Source & Placeholder Kit

Gather real material & create placeholders

What to do: Gather public info from the client's website, Facebook, and Google, then create placeholders for missing data.
How:
  • Grab business name, hours, contact from their website
  • Copy 3-5 public reviews from Facebook
  • Download public photos (store, products, team)
  • Fill gaps with realistic placeholders (e.g., "Indicative price: $2,450")
Output: /source/ and /assets/ folders with all real material organized.
⚠️ Remember: Use public info only. Never assert a specific untrue fact (wrong final price = bad).

🎨 Phase 2: Build (1.5-2 hours)

Prompt 3 — Competitor Look & Feel

Set the visual direction

What to do: Analyze how competitors look premium and define a unique visual direction for your client.
How:
  • Look at 3-5 competitors in their industry
  • Note colors, typography, hero imagery
  • Define YOUR unique direction (bold, local, premium)
  • Choose one accent color, type system, and vibe
Output: Claude will provide a crisp visual direction doc you'll reference for the next steps.

Prompt 4 — Design System & Scaffold

Set up the project structure

What to do: Create a Next.js app with design tokens and global styles.
How:
  • Claude scaffolds Next.js + TypeScript + Tailwind
  • Sets up colors, fonts (Bebas Neue + Inter)
  • Creates header, footer, and demo marker
  • Adds noindex tag (keeps it private)
Output: A running Next.js app with consistent styling ready for pages.

⭐ Prompt 5 — Homepage (WOW Moment)

🛑 PAUSE HERE AFTER THIS PROMPT

What to do: Build the hero homepage that closes the deal.
How:
  • Bold hero section (brand + locality + strong image)
  • Quick intro to the business
  • Two clear paths: "Shop Now" & "Enquire"
  • Workshop/servicing section (builds trust)
  • Real reviews/testimonials
  • Contact + hours
⚠️ CRITICAL: Stop here. Open the preview. Review the homepage. Does it look premium and impressive? If YES, continue. If NO, ask Claude to refine it before moving on.

Prompt 6 — Shop Page (Store Path)

Build product category page

What to do: Build ONE product category page to demonstrate the ecommerce path.
How:
  • Create product grid (e.g., Helmets, Gear, Apparel)
  • Add product images, names, and indicative prices
  • Style "Add to Cart" buttons (visual only — no real checkout)
  • Match homepage design
Output: Polished product page that LOOKS like a real store but is visual only.

Prompt 7 — Enquiry Page (High-Value Path)

Build enquiry page for expensive items

What to do: Build a page for high-value items (motorcycles, equipment, vehicles) with NO checkout.
How:
  • Premium specs-style layout (not a cart)
  • Showcase 3-5 key items with specs
  • Add "Enquire", "Finance Enquiry", "Test Ride" buttons
  • NO "Buy Now" or checkout buttons
  • Optional: Simple enquiry form
Output: Page showing the sales path for high-ticket items (make enquiring easy, don't force a sale).

Prompt 8 — About/Workshop (Optional Trust Page)

Build local authority page

What to do: Build a trust-building page (optional, if time allows).
How:
  • Describe their workshop/servicing
  • Share their local story (years in business, location)
  • Add trust signals (reviews, authorised dealer status)
  • Use indicative content where facts are missing
Output: Page that positions the client as a trusted local expert, not just an online store.

🚀 Phase 3: Deploy & Pitch (30-45 min)

Prompt 9 — Polish Pass

Final QA before deployment

What to do: Final quality check before deploying to Vercel.
Checklist:
  • ✓ All images optimized & loading fast
  • ✓ Mobile layout is flawless
  • ✓ Every link works, hover states smooth
  • ✓ Enquiry items have NO checkout buttons
  • ✓ Demo marker visible on every page
  • ✓ noindex tag is present
  • ✓ Spelling & grammar correct (Australian English)
Output: Claude flags any remaining issues. Fix them before moving to Prompt 10.

Prompt 10 — Deploy & Ready to Pitch

Go live on Vercel, then close the deal

What to do: Deploy to Vercel and prep your pitch.
How to Deploy:
  • From the /website folder, run: vercel
  • Get a preview URL (looks like: https://project-name.vercel.app)
  • Confirm noindex is live (keep it private)
  • Pre-load the URL before the meeting
How to Run the Pitch:
  • Step 1: Open on their business, live. Say: "Here's what MNA Moto Supplies could look like online."
  • Step 2: Walk the two paths: "A customer buys a helmet here. Someone after a $15k bike enquires here — no awkward checkout."
  • Step 3: Point to Hamilton locality, workshop, reviews. "This positions you as the trusted local expert."
  • Step 4: Be honest: "This is an indicative demo built from your public info. The real build uses your exact photos and stock."
  • Step 5: Close: "To make it real, we take a deposit and collect your content. Your live site follows this direction."
Have ready before the pitch:
  • Project fee
  • Deposit amount
  • Care plan retainer (monthly)
  • Timeline ("Live in 2 weeks once we have your content")
🎯 Result: They say yes, you collect the deposit, then start the real build with their actual photos and content.
